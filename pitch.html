The idea in one line

An AI that predicts real-world events, argues with itself, and proves it’s right before risking real money.

There are websites where people bet on whether real things will happen — “will it hit 88° in New York today?”, “who wins the election?” oddsloop makes its own honest guess at the odds, then argues against itself like a tough critic, and only bets when its case holds up. Every guess is checked against what actually happens — so we know if we can trust it.

Why this exists

Sometimes the odds are wrong. A careful, honest guesser can spot it.

On these betting sites, real money sets the odds on real questions. The crowd is usually right — but not always. The gap between a careful guess and a lazy one is the whole opportunity.

🎯 The chance

Find questions where a careful estimate disagrees with the odds — and the disagreement is backed by evidence, not wishful thinking.

🤖 The catch

You can’t trust an AI that just sounds sure of itself. It has to prove it’s right, again and again, first.

🔁 The fix

A cycle that guesses, grades itself, and improves — using fake money until it has truly earned trust.

The cycle

Six steps, running over and over.

The design that keeps it honest

Two separate parts. One can’t fool the other.

The system is split on purpose, so the part that wants to bet can never quietly lean on the part that guesses.

The thinker

Gathers information and makes honest guesses. Its only job is to be right — it never sees the money.

The banker

Takes those guesses and does the cold math — how big a bet, what the limits are. Its only job is discipline.

The AI guesses. Rules handle the money. It always argues against itself. Everything is public — a live scoreboard.

How we know it’s working

We don’t trust confidence. We trust a track record.

The test is simple: when it says something is 70% likely, does it actually happen about 70% of the time? We check that on every guess and keep score. A lower score means sharper guesses.

Why weather first

Weather questions get answered every single day — the fastest way to get graded. Hundreds of results a week means it learns quickly before moving to slower, bigger questions.

Careful on purpose

Boring where the money is.

The clever part is the judgment. The money part is deliberately simple and cautious.

🧾 Costs count

It only bets when there’s a real advantage after fees. A bet that only wins before costs gets skipped.

📏 Small and capped

Every bet is sized by rule, kept small, with hard limits on how much can ride at once.

📄 Practice first

Not one real dollar until the track record proves it. Practice mode is locked — the AI can’t switch it off.
