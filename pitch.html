The pitch in one line

An AI that forecasts markets, argues with itself, and proves its edge before risking a dollar.

oddsloop reads real-world prediction markets, forms its own honest probability for each, then attacks its own reasoning like a skeptical trading desk. It only bets where the case survives — and every call is scored against reality so we know, with numbers, whether to trust it.

Why this exists

Prediction markets price the future. Most people bet on a hunch.

On markets like Kalshi, real money sets the odds on real questions — “will NYC hit 88° today?”, “who wins the nomination?”. Those prices are usually smart, but not always. The gap between a careful, calibrated forecast and a crowd’s guess is the opportunity.

🎯 The edge

Find markets where a disciplined estimate disagrees with the price — and the disagreement is earned, not wishful.

🤖 The catch

You can’t trust an AI that just sounds confident. Confidence has to be proven against outcomes first.

🔁 The answer

A loop that predicts, scores itself, and learns — betting fake money until the edge is real.

The loop

Six steps, running on repeat.

Tap a step to see what it does. It runs continuously — data in, judgment, disciplined sizing, and a scorecard back out.

The design that makes it trustworthy

Two separate agents. One can’t justify the other.

The system is split on purpose, so the part that wants to bet can never quietly tune the part that forecasts.

The Brain

Gathers evidence and produces honest probabilities. Its job is to be right, not to be profitable. It never sees the sizing.

The Body

Takes those probabilities and does the cold math — edge after fees, position size, risk limits. Its job is discipline, not opinion.

AI judges. Code sizes the money. Adversarial by default — every thesis gets attacked. Fully transparent — public live dashboard.

How we know it works

We don’t bet on confidence. We bet on proven calibration.

Calibration means: when the model says 70%, the thing should happen about 70% of the time. We measure that on every single prediction — bet or not — with a Brier score (lower is sharper).

Why weather first

Weather markets resolve every day — the fastest possible feedback. Hundreds of scored outcomes per week means the loop learns quickly before touching slower, higher-stakes markets.

Risk, on purpose

Boring where it counts.

The interesting part is the judgment. The money part is deliberately mechanical and conservative.

🧾 Fee-aware

Edge is measured after trading fees. A thesis that only wins before costs doesn’t trade.

📏 Sized by rule

Fractional-Kelly stakes with hard caps on exposure and drawdown. No gut-sized bets.

📄 Paper first

Not one real dollar until calibration is proven. Paper mode is locked; no agent can flip it.
